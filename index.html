I'm Andy Bayles

...a Web Designer and Developer from Houston, Texas.

Since serving as Webmaster for Clements HS Thespian Troupe #3689 in Sugar Land, Texas, I’ve claimed something of a fascination with creating expressive content and tools able to find use and appreciation anywhere in the world. During my time at the University of Houston, I taught myself what would eventually become a foundation in graphic design, web design, and web programming, in the process picking up a range of skills, plus the knowledge and conceptual framework necessary to take advantage of modern development tools.

As a web developer, I strive to not only deliver an attractive, intuitive, and effective user experience on time and within available resources, but to make the process by which a project is concieved, planned, and run as accessible and understandable as possible. Whether you’re starting a business, attempting to decentralize individual pieces of a larger operation, or just need a way to generate exposure for your own work online, it's my responsibility to produce solutions the likes of which will make you question why you ever sufficed without them.

As of right now, my career goals include securing a position from which I can apply and focus on the practical and creative aspects of web development in my day-to-day work as part of an enthusiastic, knowledgeable, and personable team from which I can learn and receive feedback and inspiration from. I'd like to further build upon my experience with current web design trends and their application across today’s broad spectrum of platforms, while becoming ever more knowledgeable with the use of development tools, industry practices, and our increasingly interwoven online environment as a whole.

Technical Skills

From which I derive my Geek Cred.

PHP / MySQL (CodeIgniter)

My favorite aspect of the craft. Among other things, teaching myself PHP with CodeIgniter helped to chisel Seperation of Concerns into my brain via the prism of a Model-View-Controller framework.

HTML5 / CSS3

Becoming versed in the crux of the web's visual structure - apart from being a vital part of a developer's education - introduced me to the advantages of code optimization for resources, in this case bandwidth.

JavaScript / jQuery

With its relatively loose syntax and data structures, I think of JavaScript as having been more of a challange to grasp; though its extreme flexibility and the sheer usefulness of the jQuery library more than made up for it.

Wordpress

The web's most popular CMS and a local friend of a friend's brainchild. I've found Wordpress to be most useful for clients in need of quick deployment with ability to self-maintain with minimum outside assistance.

Database Design, Administration

Designing, constructing, and maintaining the MySQL databases underlying my projects has done much to make me comfortable with the practices necessary to establish workable relationships between data, as well as how to usefully model multidimensional information.

Graphic Design

I found myself originally drawn to web design in the late 90's via its aesthetic aspects, and later expanded into Illustrator after building my earliest sites with Photoshop. My fascination with graphic design inspired me to hone my skills concocting branding solutions for my employers, some of which can be viewed below.
